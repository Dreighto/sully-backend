My honest read

A2 (folded ribbon S) is the strongest candidate. It works at every size, has a real letterform anchor (the S), and the cream-on-pink palette has the warmth the brief asked for. It's also the closest to a flagship app icon — sits naturally next to Things, Linear, or Notion on an iOS home screen.

B2 (bold crescent) is the alternative if you want vibe over letterform. It's atmospheric — a quiet always-present companion mark. Weakness: less distinctive at very small sizes (the crescent fills the bounding box, which can read busy).

C2 (brushstroke) is decent but loses the letterform legibility A2 keeps. Useful as a fallback direction if you want personal/handmade energy.

If A2 doesn't land, the cheapest next iteration is 2–3 A-variants with different ribbon angles (more vertical, more horizontal, less curl on the bottom). Tell me which direction to push.
