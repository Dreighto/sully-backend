07 · RESEARCH

Hermes 3 evaluation — should we switch?

You asked. Deep research on NousResearch's Hermes 3 / 4 family vs your current setup. Bottom line: stay on Qwen 3 14B as primary, with two specific reasons + a model to watch. Full reasoning below.
"Hermes 3 8B is a measurable reasoning downgrade from Qwen 3 14B. You can paint personality on with a system prompt + memory; you can't paint IQ on. The Apache 2.0 vs Llama Community License gap is a real future-proofing concern if you share or commercialize."

Stay on Qwen 3 14B RECOMMENDED

Higher IQ at your VRAM ceiling. Apache 2.0 — clean for sharing/commercializing. Richer fine-tuning ecosystem (Unsloth, Axolotl, LLaMA-Factory all have first-class Qwen recipes).

Action: No change to companion-v1.

Hermes 3 8B as persona module OPTIONAL

If you want roleplay-style sessions or extra-warm chat, pull hermes3:8b-llama3.1-q4_K_M as a secondary model. Route to it deliberately — not as default.

VRAM: ~4-5GB at Q4. Fits comfortably alongside companion-v1.

Watch Hermes 4 14B (Qwen-based) RE-EVAL 60-90d

Released Aug 2025. Apache 2.0 inherited from Qwen 3 base. Hybrid reasoning. Not on Ollama yet (Featherless API only). If it hits Ollama + benchmarks well, this is the natural upgrade.

Status: Watch ollama.com/library/hermes4.

THE DATA — concrete benchmarks (source-cited only)

Qwen 3 14B beats Hermes 3 70B on GPQA + coding index. (LLMBase)

Ai2 third-party eval: Hermes 3 8B underperforms Llama 3.1 8B Instruct on official eval template. (Interconnects / Nathan Lambert) — this is the most load-bearing finding.

LoRA on 5060 Ti 16GB: Llama 3.1 8B QLoRA = ~6GB training memory; Unsloth fits Qwen3 30B-A3B in 17.5GB. QLoRA on Qwen 3 14B is feasible on your card.

License: Hermes 3 inherits Llama Community License (field-of-use restrictions, EU-user exclusions, NOT OSI-open). Qwen 3 = Apache 2.0 (clean for any use).

The "learning" angle: What makes Sully feel like she learns is the memory layers you already have (Layer 1 working summary, Layer 3 semantic facts) — not the base model. 80% of "learning" is RAG. The other 20% is the eventual graduate-to-student fine-tune, which we'd do on Qwen 3 14B via Unsloth QLoRA on your 5060 Ti. Apache license keeps the door open.
